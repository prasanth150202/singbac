4-Steps to Register your Company in Singapore (2024)

Step 1: Choose a Company Name & Business Activity

It is a legal requirement to register a private limited company if you would like to operate a business in Singapore. Sole-Proprietor and Partnership entity types are only available to Local Residents. The minimum age for registration as an individual director or shareholder is 18 years old.

Foreigners are allowed to have 100% ownership of the Singapore company, as an individual or using another foreign entity.

The Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA) is the regulator of business registration, financial reporting, public accountants, and corporate service providers in Singapore.

You will have to appoint an agency to register your company with ACRA. This process can be done entirely online and is typically completed within 2 to 3 working days.

The first step of registering a local company is to choose a company name and submit a name application. Your proposed company name cannot be identical to an existing name or contain prohibited and undesirable words.

Read or watch ACRA’s 4-minute guide on choosing a suitable business name and checking on its availability.

Some name applications may be referred to Referral Authorities for approval (e.g. if your proposed business name has the word “school”, it may be referred to the Ministry of Education). The processing time for referred applications may take between 14 to 60 days.

As part of the name application, you must indicate the primary and secondary activities of your business by choosing the most relevant Singapore Standard Industrial Classification (SSIC) code corresponding to your intended business activity.

You are allowed a maximum of 2 different business activities for each company.

Some business activities may also require additional licences or permits before you can start your operations. Find out more on GoBusiness for licences and permits.

Step 2: Appoint a Corporate Service Provider & Make Payment

A corporate service provider is a filing agent or qualified individual licensed to transact with ACRA on behalf of another person. These may include a law firm, accounting firm, or corporate secretarial firm that provides statutory filing services for its clients. A Qualified Individual is an individual who performs these transactions and may include advocates and solicitors, public accountants, chartered accountants, and corporate secretarial agents.

Typically, to save costs, you would want to appoint a corporate secretarial agent for a simple structure company setup instead of using a law firm, which is only for more complicated company types meant for Multi-Nationals or Family Offices. There are more than 250 secretarial agencies in Singapore, most of whom offer company registration services for foreigners to be completed entirely online, with payment made via credit card.

As a foreigner, you would be required to take up the following services and pay the associated fees to complete the registration of a private limited company in Singapore:

1. Company Incorporation Fee (S$350)

This is a mandatory government-imposed fee for the cost of company registration, and is paid to ACRA by your appointed agency.

2. Corporate Secretarial Fee (Starts from S$300 onwards)

Every private limited company registered in Singapore is required under law to appoint a corporate Secretary to file your annual returns yearly. Agencies typically charge a fee of S$300 onwards yearly for the provision of corporate secretarial services. This fee may be higher, depending on the structure of your company, number of shareholders, etc. This fee may also include or exclude a government-mandated fee of S$60 for filing of annual returns.

3. Nominee Director (Starts from S$1,200)

Every company in Singapore is required to appoint at least 1 local resident director in the company. This director does not hold shares and is typically not involved in the day-to-day operations of your company. Typically, this is a nominee role, and all agencies provide this service. The Nominee Director is held responsible for all compliance-related matters in your company. Hence, agencies may collect a security deposit to provide this service or request you to compulsorily take up one of their tax and accounting packages.

4. Local Registered Address (Starts from S$200)

A registered office address refers to the place where all communications and notices to the company may be sent, and the place where the company’s register and records are kept. The address must be operational and accessible to the public during normal office hours. This cannot be a virtual or PO Box address. Agencies provide this service coupled with a digital mailroom service, where any mails received are scanned and uploaded to your email address for ease of viewing.

If you have or plan to get a physical office or co-working space in Singapore, you may choose to use your office address as the registered address instead.

5. Share Capital

The minimum share capital for registration of a private limited company is S$1.00. This does not mean you need to inject S$1 into your company’s bank account. You just need to declare this amount to ACRA during registration and issue 1 share to yourself as the shareholder.

You may later top up the share capital at any time to the amount you require or increase it to a higher amount if you require financing from local banks or investors.

The share capital declaration does not incur any additional charges.

In summary, the total fees to register a private limited company for foreigners with a corporate service provider range from S$1,000 to S$3,000, depending on your choice of services and service providers.

To avoid paying too much, make sure to ask your chosen agency if the fee includes all costs or is exclusive of some mandatory government or third-party charges.

Step 3: Register Your Company

Your chosen corporate service provider will file the application for company registration with ACRA on your behalf.

The registration of your private limited company will be completed in approximately 2 to 3 working days if all required documents and information are submitted, including:

  • Identification documents of shareholders and directors.
  • Confirmation of company name and business activities.
  • Approval of any licences or permits required for your business activities.
  • Proof of local address.
  • Declaration of compliance with the legal requirements and regulations.

Once the company is registered, you will receive the following documents:

  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • Company Business Profile
  • Company Constitution

These documents are important for opening a bank account and for other business operations.

Important Note: The registration process will require your company to have a local company secretary, local registered address, and a local resident director in place before submission. If any of these are missing, the application may be rejected or delayed.

Step 4: Open a Corporate Bank Account

With the company registered, the next step is to open a corporate bank account.

Here’s what you need to prepare for the account opening:

  • Company registration documents (Certificate of Incorporation, Business Profile, Constitution).
  • Identification documents of company directors and shareholders.
  • Proof of residential address of company directors and shareholders.
  • Additional documents requested by the bank (e.g., business plan, details of business activities).

Different banks have different requirements and procedures. Some banks may allow you to open an account online, while others may require a physical visit.

Ensure that you compare the services and fees of different banks to choose one that best fits your needs.

Once your bank account is opened, you will receive the account details and can start operating your business financially.
