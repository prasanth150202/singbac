Your Step-by-Step Guide to Setting Up a Foreign Company in Singapore

1

Fulfill Legal Requirements


  • Corporate Secretary: Appoint a secretary to handle compliance, file annual returns, organize AGMs, and manage dividends.
  • Nominee Director:Hire a Singaporean resident to fulfill the director role. They handle legal responsibilities but not business decisions.
  • Registered Address: Obtain a local address for your company’s legal documents and correspondence.

    • 2

    Register Your Company


  • Complete identity verification for all key personnel.
  • Provide necessary proof of address and company details.
  • Decide on your paid-up capital (as little as S$1).
  • Submit and sign incorporation documents electronically.

    • 3

    Open a Business Bank Account

  • Work with Singaporean banks to open an account remotely.
  • Banks will conduct online KYC interviews and handle document exchanges via courier.
  • Account approval is determined by the banks.

    • 4

    Set Up Your Accounting Schedule

  • Prepare and organize your tax reports and financial statements.
  • Submit your Estimated Chargeable Income, financial statements, and annual tax returns on time to avoid penalties.

    • 5

    Apply for an Employment Pass

  • Obtain an Employment Pass to live and work in Singapore.
  • Submit your CV, professional background, and company details.
  • The EP application takes 3-6 months and is valid for 1-2 years, with renewal options.

    • 6

    Congratulations, all done!


  • You’ve successfully navigated the setup process.
  • Your company is now legally registered and compliant.
  • Your business account is ready for transactions.
  • Your accounting and tax filings are set up.
  • You’re prepared to live and work in Singapore.
